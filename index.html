I'm Pavindran Velalagan — a third-year Computer Engineering undergraduate at the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka. I build things that sit at the intersection of software, hardware, and intelligence.

Most recently I worked as an ML Engineering Apprentice at Cogntix, where I dug into text extraction from complex document layouts, CV classification models, and cloud deployment on AWS. Before that, I served as a casual instructor helping undergraduates find their footing with Python and Object-Oriented Programming.

My projects range from a closed-loop IoT medical device that regulates IV drip flow rates, to a full-stack event management platform with an AI-powered budget bot, to a published research paper on Vision Transformers for tomato leaf disease recognition. I'm drawn to problems where both the thinking and the making matter.

Outside of engineering I'm actively involved in the IEEE Computer Society, AerogenZ Aviation Club, and Perabeats Media Society — where I've spent time as a videographer, editor, and webmaster.

I'm always open to interesting projects, research collaborations, and good conversations. Feel free to reach out.
